# Cartridge Game Ideas

These are small, offline-friendly cartridge concepts shaped by the swipe feedback:
fast local context, concrete NYC-ish situations, and replayable rounds from a
bundled seed deck rather than a live backend.

1. StoopSwipe
   Swipe through curb finds, stoop-sale photos, and free piles. Score comes from
   spotting value, avoiding traps, and building the best walking route before
   other fictional players claim the finds.

2. Stoop Sale Gold Rush
   A timed neighborhood treasure hunt where every stop has a weird bundle,
   haggling risk, and carrying-capacity tradeoff. The fun is turning junk into a
   coherent haul.

3. Rentle
   A daily rent-guessing game. The cartridge shows listing clues one at a time:
   borough, train distance, square footage, listing language, amenities, then the
   player guesses the rent in as few clues as possible.

4. Free Today
   A logistics puzzle about free events, giveaways, samples, and expiring offers.
   Build a day plan with time windows, transit friction, and hidden disappointment
   risks.

5. Rent vs Anywhere
   A comparison game where players decide whether a NYC apartment is a better
   deal than strange alternatives elsewhere: a lake cabin, a mall-adjacent studio,
   a tiny house lease, a luxury closet, or a fake listing.

6. Last Call
   A closing-time route game. You have limited time to hit late-night food,
   pharmacies, trains, bathrooms, and friend meetups before everything shuts.

7. Lunch Special Radar
   A deduction game for finding the best lunch special from partial signs,
   neighborhood hints, price anchors, and crowd signals. Wrong guesses waste
   time; good reads chain into streak bonuses.

8. Price Is Wrong
   A quick party game of absurd local price guessing: broker fee, bodega basket,
   storage unit, museum snack, dog walker, repair quote. Close guesses score, but
   confident wrong answers can be funnier than right ones.

9. Group Table
   A cooperative planning game where each player has private constraints for
   dinner: budget, dietary rules, noise tolerance, distance, hidden vetoes. Win by
   finding a table everyone will actually accept.

10. Who Said It?
    A quote-identification game using a bundled cast of local archetypes,
    apartment listings, review snippets, landlord texts, group-chat messages, and
    overheard lines. The player guesses source and context.
